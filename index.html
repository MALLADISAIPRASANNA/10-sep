Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India."

Early Life

Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Kalam came from humble beginnings. He studied physics and aerospace engineering and joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).

Achievements

  • Led India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998
  • Developed the Agni and Prithvi missiles
  • Authored inspirational books like "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds"
  • Served as President of India from 2002 to 2007

Legacy

Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture. He is remembered for his humility, vision, and dedication to youth and education.
